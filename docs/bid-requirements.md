"Architects are good at designing buildings, its deeply frustrating that we spend so long writing bids"

First bottleneck to getting revenue for practices
Biggest reason that projects are won is because you have an *in* with the project

## problems that need to be solved
clients expect it to be a lot more bespoke than it was (more competitive, bar is being raised)
Streamline data storage of bid answers so they become a lot easier to retrieve, and prevents repetition of work.
Ridiculous number of questions from clients, sometimes written by AI.
Bid needs to be quite visual, easy as possible for the clients to ingest and score against criteria (prediction of criteria)
There's a whole decision making process before the practice even selects the bids, this has to feel easy and quick. Figuring out the ratio of time-put-in:size-of-commission.
Commonly under-estimate how long the bid process is gonna take, commonly gets done by employees working in the evenings (e.g., Simon worked out a bid took over $6k of just his time)
Feedback review process, where data from the feedback for the client is used to extract the highest performing aspects of previous bids

## Process
AI Process needs to be careful, first structure, first draft, lots of human editing (Guernsey Master Plan for Local Government)

### ?First Stage, can take 2days-week (SQ)
sometimes 2 stage, first stage is credentials so you send all the authentication things, quality assurance stuff. (bid team, one person assigned to a bid)
when it comes to bespoke quality questions, they will try and find previous answers that we've done on previous bids.

### ITT (Invitation to Tender)
Senior staff then sit down to answer the questions
Multiple 6-7k words sometimes
