# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

The primary users are architecture practice staff involved in bids: bid leads, directors, and senior staff who need to inspect, correct, and align around a proposed AI-assisted bid workflow. A secondary audience is Arka Works staff evaluating the approach, judgment, and prototype direction.

They use the HTML slide set as an interactive workshop board: a shared surface for discussing the current bid process, checking assumptions, identifying pain points, and shaping a small prototype agent workflow.

## Product Purpose

This artifact exists to make a messy bid process visible, discussable, and correctable. It should help the practice confirm what is true, challenge what is vague, and agree on a practical prototype scope without pretending to solve the whole bid system at once.

Success looks like a clearer shared model of the bid workflow, a credible agent-design wedge, and visible trust that the approach is careful, commercially aware, and human-accountable.

## Brand Personality

Clear, critical, constructive.

The tone should feel like a thoughtful workshop facilitator: direct enough to name weak spots, careful enough not to overclaim, and practical enough to turn ambiguity into next actions.

## Anti-references

This should not look like:

- a generic SaaS deck
- an academic report
- a flashy AI demo
- a dense corporate PowerPoint
- an over-polished consultancy theatre piece that hides uncertainty
- a generic agentic-AI hype presentation

## Design Principles

1. Make the workflow inspectable: show process, decisions, assumptions, risks, and feedback loops as things the client can correct.
2. Prototype the wedge, not the universe: distinguish today’s small agent demo from future platform improvements.
3. Keep human accountability visible: AI supports intake, mapping, drafting, and review; people make bid and professional judgments.
4. Prefer critical usefulness over positivity: the artifact should help identify what is weak, generic, missing, risky, or unclear.
5. Design for conversation: every slide should afford a response, correction, decision, or next question.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Design for laptop and projector use. Use strong contrast, readable type sizes, reduced-motion-safe interactions, and avoid relying on color alone to communicate meaning.
